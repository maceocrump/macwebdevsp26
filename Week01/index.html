Van’s Chocolate Chip Cookies

A sophisticated take on the classic cookie featuring nutty brown butter, rich dark chocolate chunks, and a 48-hour chill time for maximum flavor development.

Ingredients

Brown Butter

  • 2 sticks unsalted butter
  • 2 ice cubes

Dough

  • 1/2 cup white granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups dark brown sugar
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (approx. 2.5 bars) 65-70% dark chocolate, roughly chopped

Instructions

1. Prepare the Chocolate

Chop the chocolate bars thoroughly into uneven larger and smaller chunks. Collect all pieces, including the "chocolate dust," into a measuring cup and set aside.

2. Make the Dough

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine dark brown sugar, white sugar, salt, and baking soda. Mix until it reaches the consistency of kinetic sand.
  3. In a saucepan over medium-low heat, cook the butter until it reaches a golden amber color with dark specks and a nutty aroma.
  4. Transfer butter to a heat-proof bowl. Once slightly cooled, add ice cubes one at a time to bring the temperature down to approximately 100°F.
  5. Pour butter into the sugar mixture and mix until fully incorporated.
  6. Add eggs and vanilla; whisk vigorously until the mixture resembles a wet cake batter.
  7. Gradually fold in the flour until no white streaks remain.
  8. Fold in the chocolate chunks until evenly distributed.

3. Refrigeration (Crucial Step)

For best results, choose one of the following methods and refrigerate for 48 hours:

  • Best: Scoop into craggly, imperfect balls, wrap individually in plastic, and chill.
  • Good: Scoop into balls on a parchment-lined sheet and cover the entire sheet tightly with plastic.
  • Standard: Cover the mixing bowl tightly with plastic wrap and chill the dough as a whole.

4. Baking

  1. Place dough balls on parchment-lined baking sheets.
  2. Bake at 350°F for 15–20 minutes. The centers should still look slightly soft.
  3. Let cookies cool on the sheet for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Transfer to a wire rack and cool for another 5–10 minutes before serving.
