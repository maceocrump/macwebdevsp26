Van’s Signature Cookies

A masterclass in texture: Brown butter, handmade toffee, and dark chocolate.

Choose Your Ingredients

Version A: With Toffee

Toffee:
  • 1 stick unsalted butter
  • 1 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
Brown Butter:
  • 2 sticks high-quality butter
  • 2+ ice cubes
Dough & Mix-ins:
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups dark brown sugar
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 eggs + 2 tsp quality vanilla
  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 1-1.25 cups chopped dark chocolate (65-70%)
  • Optional: 2 tsp espresso powder
  • Optional: Flaky salt for topping

Version B: Pure Chocolate

Brown Butter:
  • 2 sticks high-quality butter
  • 2+ ice cubes
Dough & Mix-ins:
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups dark brown sugar
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 eggs + 2 tsp quality vanilla
  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 2 cups chopped dark chocolate (65-70%)
  • Optional: 2 tsp espresso powder
  • Optional: Flaky salt for topping

Preparation

Step 1: The Toffee (Make Ahead)

Combine light brown sugar, salt, and butter in a skillet over medium-low heat. Stir until melted and caramel-like. Bring to a slow, steady bubble (300°F or dark golden brown). Pour onto parchment and freeze for 30 minutes until brittle.

Step 2: The Chocolate

Chop bars into uneven chunks. Save every bit of "chocolate dust" to add to the dough later.

Instructions

  1. Cream the Sugars: Combine dark brown sugar, white sugar, salt, baking soda, and (optional) espresso powder. Mix until it feels like kinetic sand.
  2. Brown the Butter: Cook butter over medium-low heat until golden amber with dark specks and a nutty smell. Transfer to a bowl and add ice cubes one at a time until the temperature hits 100°F.
  3. Emulsify: Mix butter into sugar until fully incorporated. Add eggs and vanilla, whisking vigorously until it looks like a wet cake batter.
  4. Combine: Fold in flour until no white streaks remain. Stir in chocolate chunks (and smashed toffee pieces if using).

The 48-Hour Chill

BEST: Scoop into craggly, imperfect balls. Individually wrap in plastic. Refrigerate for 48 hours.
GOOD: Scoop onto a parchment-lined sheet. Wrap the entire sheet tightly with plastic. Refrigerate for 48 hours.
STANDARD: Cover the mixing bowl tightly with plastic. Refrigerate for 48 hours.

Baking

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Place dough on parchment-lined sheets.
  3. Bake for 15–20 minutes. If centers look slightly soft/raw at 15 mins, they are likely perfect.
  4. Cool on the sheet for 2–3 minutes, then move to a wire rack for 5–10 minutes.
  5. Top with flaky salt while warm.
