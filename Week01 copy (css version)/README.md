# Van’s Cookies  
**Prep time:** 45 mins | **Chill time:** 48 hours | **Bake time:** 15-20 mins  
  
## I. Ingredient Lists  
## A. Option 1: With Homemade Toffee  
* **Toffee (Make Ahead)**  
    * 1 stick unsalted butter  
    * 1 cup light brown sugar (tightly packed)  
    * 1 teaspoon kosher salt  
* **Brown Butter**  
    * 2 sticks high-quality unsalted butter (e.g., Kerrygold)  
    * 2+ ice cubes (adjust based on temperature)  
* **Dough & Mix-ins**  
    * ½ cup white granulated sugar  
    * 1 ½ cups dark brown sugar  
    * 2 teaspoons kosher salt  
    * ½ teaspoon baking soda  
    * 2 eggs  
    * 2 teaspoons high-quality vanilla extract  
    * 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour  
    * 1–1.25 cups (approx. 1.5 bars) dark chocolate (65-70%), chopped  
## B. Option 2: Without Toffee  
* **Brown Butter**  
    * 2 sticks high-quality unsalted butter  
    * 2+ ice cubes  
* **Dough & Mix-ins**  
    * ½ cup white granulated sugar  
    * 1 ½ cups dark brown sugar  
    * 2 teaspoons kosher salt  
    * ½ teaspoon baking soda  
    * 2 eggs  
    * 2 teaspoons high-quality vanilla extract  
    * 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour  
    * 2 cups (approx. 2.5 bars) dark chocolate (65-70%), chopped  
## C. Optional Additions (Both Versions)  
* **Depth:** 2 teaspoons espresso powder (mix into dry ingredients)  
* **Finish:** Flaky sea salt (sprinkle after baking)  
  
## II. Preparation Steps  
## 1. The Toffee  
**Note:** Complete this at least 30 minutes before starting the dough.  
1. Combine light brown sugar, salt, and butter in a medium skillet over medium-low heat.  
2. Melt until it reaches a caramel-like consistency.  
3. Bring to a slow, steady bubble. If using a thermometer, heat to **300°F**. Otherwise, look for a dark golden brown color and a rich (not burnt) aroma.  
4. Pour onto a parchment-lined sheet and freeze for ~30 minutes until it cracks when bent.  
## 2. The Chocolate  
1. Chop chocolate bars thoroughly into uneven chunks (aim for a mix of large shards and small bits).  
2. **Do not discard the chocolate "dust"**—measure it and include it all in the dough.  
  
## III. Dough Instructions  
1. **Prep Dry Base:** In a large bowl, combine dark brown sugar, white sugar, salt, baking soda, and espresso powder (if using). Mix until it reaches a "kinetic sand" consistency.  
2. **Brown the Butter:** In a saucepan over medium-low heat, cook 2 sticks of butter until golden amber. Look for dark specks and a nutty scent.  
3. **Cool & Emulsify:** Transfer butter to a heat-proof bowl. Add ice cubes one at a time until the mixture cools to roughly **100°F**.  
4. **Incorporate:** Pour butter into the sugar mixture. Mix until fully incorporated with no separated butter on the sides.  
5. **Wet Ingredients:** Add eggs and vanilla. Whisk vigorously until the mixture looks like a wet cake batter.  
6. **Flour:** Add flour (gradually or all at once). Mix until absolutely no white streaks remain.  
7. **Fold:** Stir in chocolate chunks (and toffee bits if using) until evenly distributed.  
  
## IV. The 48-Hour Refrigeration  
*Choose one method based on your preference for texture:*  
* **Tier 1 (Best):** Scoop into craggly, imperfect balls. Individually wrap in plastic wrap. Refrigerate 48 hours.  
* **Tier 2 (Good):** Scoop into imperfect balls on a parchment-lined sheet. Cover the entire sheet tightly with plastic wrap (no air pockets). Refrigerate 48 hours.  
* **Tier 3 (Standard):** Keep dough in the mixing bowl. Cover tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate 48 hours.  
  
## V. Baking & Finishing  
1. **Preheat:** Set oven to **350°F**.  
2. **Arrange:** Place dough balls on parchment-lined baking sheets.  
3. **Bake:** Bake for **15 minutes**. If the centers look slightly soft or raw, you can go up to **20 minutes**, but do not overbake.  
4. **Rest:** Allow cookies to cool on the hot sheet for 2–3 minutes.  
5. **Cool:** Transfer to a wire rack for 5–10 minutes.  
6. **Serve:** Finished! Sprinkle with flaky salt if desired.
